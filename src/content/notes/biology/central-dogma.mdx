DNA → RNA (transcription) → protein (translation). Reads from a sequencer are stored in
[[FASTQ Format]] before alignment.

- **Transcription**: RNA polymerase reads a DNA template strand and synthesizes a
  complementary mRNA strand.
- **Translation**: ribosomes read mRNA codons (triplets of bases) and assemble the
  corresponding amino acid chain, guided by tRNA.

Exceptions exist (reverse transcription in retroviruses runs RNA → DNA), which is
part of why "dogma" is a slightly misleading name in hindsight.
